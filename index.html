About

I am a Computer Science PhD student at Radboud University Nijmegen. My work involves model learning techniques and their application to real-world systems. Aside from that, I am always looking to improve my knowledge on computers, programming... That is, when I'm not singing, browsing Internet overloading my mind with irrelevant information...

Research Interest

As previously mentioned, my research area is model learning and its application. In particular, my focus is on developing model learning techniques for Register Automata, formalisms that allow the representation of data flow. A side goal is to apply model learning to practical software, like network protocols.

More generally, my research interests lie in:

  • model learning algorithms and applications
  • conformance and model-based testing
  • reverse engineering
  • application of formal methods to practice
  • network protocols
